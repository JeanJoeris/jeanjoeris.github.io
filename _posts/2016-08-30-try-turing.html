--- title: Try Turing Retrospective layout: post date: 2016-08-30 ---

Last weekend I helped with Try Turing Fort Collins as a TA and on a panel of Turing students. Over two days, prospective students tried back-end and front-end engineering. As a Fort Collins native with an interest in teaching, I couldn't pass it up.

I was there on the front-end day, which allowed me to teach and learn. I've only recently learned html and css, so helping others learn these skills was daunting. Once we got started, I was able to apply general programming and debugging skills to help students. This was rewarding, and gave me confidence in what I have been learning in Turing.

As we moved into css animations and jQuery, I was into new territory. It was exciting to dive deeper into front-end. I was pleasantly surprised with how many of programming skills transferred. It was liberating to see the same skills being useful in creative and expressive ways. Applying these skills, I was able learn and teach at the same time. This drove home just how much I have learned these past 10 weeks.

As a feminist, I was glad the attendees were asking real questions about sexism, racism, and privilege in tech. As a developer and student of Turing, I was able to say they are definitely problems, but there are also people creating change. It is awesome to be studying at a place that recognizes the importance of these issues.

It was great to visit home, and the event taught me a huge amount. My only regret is not being there Saturday when they had delicious pizza from a place I used to work.
